# AI Signal Desk Product Brief

## Target reader

AI-curious builders, operators, PMs, and side-project founders who want to stay current on AI without reading noisy announcement threads all day.

The reader is looking for something that is **not generic AI news**. They want practical interpretation:

- what changed;
- why it matters;
- whether to learn, try, watch, or ignore it;
- what to do with it this week.

## Product promise

AI Signal Desk helps readers understand the AI concepts, products, repos, and workflows worth paying attention to — with clear explanations and practical next actions.

Short promise:

> Stay current on AI without drowning in hype.

## MVP scope

The first version is a static website with:

- a homepage explaining the promise;
- seeded digest cards across concepts, products, repos, and workflows;
- a weekly brief format;
- a newsletter/conversion section;
- deterministic scaffold checks so future changes are easy to validate.

## Non-goals

- Not a breaking-news feed.
- Not a generic AI newsletter clone.
- Not a social network.
- Not a paid community yet.
- Not automated publishing without human review.

## Editorial rules

Every item must answer:

1. What is it?
2. Why does it matter?
3. What should the reader do next?
4. Status: learn, try, watch, or ignore.

Prefer clear, practical explanations over hype. Do not publish an item just because it is trending.

## Content pillars

- Concepts: explain ideas like agent loops, MCP, evals, context engineering.
- Products: evaluate tools and give practical use cases.
- Repos: identify useful open-source projects and maturity level.
- Workflows: show repeatable recipes readers can copy.
- Weekly brief: summarize the highest-signal updates.

## First conversion goal

Get visitors to subscribe for a weekly practical AI brief.

Secondary goal: collect signals about which categories readers care about most.

## Success metrics

- 3 published sample posts.
- 1 weekly brief issue format.
- 25 early subscribers or testers.
- At least 5 qualitative replies about what readers want more of.

## Next build step
